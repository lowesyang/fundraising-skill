# Contributing: Stage Playbooks

**Location:** `fundraising/references/stage-playbooks/{stage}.md`

Stages: `pre-seed.md`, `seed.md`, `series-a.md`, `series-b.md`, `growth.md`, `late-stage.md`, `ipo.md`

Each playbook must include:
- Overview (typical raise, structures, timeline)
- What Investors Expect (traction benchmarks, team, market proof)
- Readiness Benchmarks with explicit RED/YELLOW/GREEN thresholds
- Typical Terms (valuation, dilution, structures)
- Key Mistakes to Avoid (3-5 stage-specific pitfalls)
- The Narrative Shift (how the pitch story changes at this stage)
- Timeline and Process
- Who to Talk To (relevant investors)

**Important:** The pre-seed playbook has a **hard gate** — if no product is launched and the team
doesn't have exceptional credentials, the verdict cannot be RAISE NOW. Do not remove this gate.

## Notes for Growth / Late / Pre-IPO / IPO Playbooks

These later-stage playbooks have a different character from early-stage ones:

- **Case studies are required.** Real examples of both successful and failed rounds (with approximate dollar amounts and dates) must anchor each playbook. These are not hypothetical — they are drawn from publicly reported data.
- **Crossover and institutional investor types** replace VC firms as the primary investor audience at late stage, pre-IPO, and IPO.
- **Governance and audit readiness** replace product-market fit as the primary readiness gate at late stage and beyond.
- **IPO playbook:** The IPO is not a fundraising round — it is a liquidity and capital market event. The playbook should reflect this distinction and focus on stock durability, not just capital raised.
- **Pre-IPO playbook:** Emphasize secondary market dynamics, employee liquidity, and the transition from private-market storytelling to public-company positioning.
- **Do not add fictional case studies.** All company references must be based on publicly reported information. If in doubt, omit rather than invent.
