# Pre-Seed Fundraising Playbook

## Overview
- **Typical raise amount:** $50K-$500K (median ~$150K-$250K)
- **Common structures:** Post-money SAFE (most common), uncapped SAFE, convertible notes; priced rounds are rare at this stage
- **Typical timeline:** 2-6 weeks from first meeting to close; can be as fast as a single meeting with angels

## What Investors Expect at This Stage
- **Traction benchmarks:** No revenue required. A working prototype, waitlist signups (500+), or letter of intent from potential customers is sufficient. Some pre-seed rounds close on a pitch deck alone.
- **Team composition:** 1-3 founders. At least one technical co-founder if building software. Strong founder-market fit (direct experience with the problem). Prior startup experience is a plus but not required.
- **Market proof needed:** Clear articulation of the problem, evidence you've talked to 20+ potential customers, and a credible theory of why now.
- **Product maturity:** Idea stage to working prototype. No launched product required, but a demo or mockup significantly helps.

## Hard Gate: Product Launch Status

**This is the most important factor in the pre-seed readiness assessment.**

A startup with no launched product and no revenue faces a hard gate on the RAISE NOW verdict:

- **RAISE NOW is only possible** if the founding team has exceptional credentials:
  - Repeat founders with previous successful exits, OR
  - Deep domain expertise (5+ years) in the exact problem space with a clear unfair advantage, OR
  - Former senior executives at directly relevant companies (e.g., ex-Stripe founding engineer building a payments company)
- **In all other cases**, the verdict should be **NOT YET** or **MAYBE** with a strong recommendation
  to build and launch an MVP first.

**Why this gate exists:** Pre-seed investors with no product data are betting entirely on team and idea.
Without exceptional team credentials, the risk premium is extremely high, terms will be unfavorable,
and the founder's time is better spent building than fundraising. A launched product with even 50-100
real users fundamentally changes the fundraising conversation.

### Product Launch Status Benchmark

| Status | Grade | Implication |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Launched with real users generating data | GREEN | Can proceed to fundraise based on traction |
| Beta / invite-only with limited users | YELLOW | Soft launch — some data available, borderline |
| Not launched — prototype or idea only | RED | Hard gate applies: need exceptional team for RAISE NOW |

## MVP-First Recommendation

When the product is not launched, always include this guidance:

> **Build first, raise second.** With modern AI development tools (Cursor, Claude Code, Replit Agent,
> v0), building a functional MVP is faster than ever — often weeks, not months. A launched product
> with 100 real users gives you dramatically better leverage than any pitch deck:
>
> - **Better terms:** Investors can evaluate real usage data, not just promises
> - **Faster close:** Data reduces perceived risk, shortening due diligence
> - **Stronger position:** You're negotiating from "users love this" not "trust me"
> - **Natural validation:** If you can't get 100 users to try your product, that's important signal
>
> **Exception:** If you're a repeat founder with exits, or have deep domain expertise that makes
> investors bet on you personally, you can raise pre-product. But even then, launching first is
> usually the better path.

## Pre-Seed Investor Focus Areas

When evaluating a pre-seed company (especially pre-launch), investors weight these factors heavily:

1. **Team credentials and track record** (50% of decision)
   - Previous startup experience, especially exits
   - Domain expertise in the problem space
   - Technical capability to build the product
   - Founder chemistry and complementary skills

2. **Idea potential and market imagination** (30% of decision)
   - Is the TAM genuinely large ($1B+)?
   - Is there a credible "why now?" — why hasn't this been built before?
   - Can this become a platform or just a feature?
   - Is there a non-obvious insight the founders have?

3. **Early validation signals** (20% of decision)
   - Customer discovery depth (interviews, insights)
   - Waitlist or LOI traction
   - Prototype quality
   - Speed of execution so far

## Readiness Benchmarks (for /before-fundraising)

These thresholds power the diagnostic grading system:

- **Metric: Product Launch Status** (HARD GATE — see above)
  - GREEN: Product launched with real users generating usage data
  - YELLOW: Beta or invite-only launch with limited users
  - RED: Not launched — prototype, mockup, or idea only (triggers hard gate on RAISE NOW verdict)

- **Metric: Customer Discovery Interviews**
  - GREEN: 30+ interviews completed with documented insights
  - YELLOW: 10-29 interviews completed
  - RED: Fewer than 10 interviews or none documented

- **Metric: Waitlist / Signups**
  - GREEN: 500+ signups or LOIs from potential customers
  - YELLOW: 100-499 signups
  - RED: Fewer than 100 or no demand validation

- **Metric: Prototype / Demo**
  - GREEN: Working prototype that demonstrates core value proposition
  - YELLOW: Clickable mockup or wireframes
  - RED: Slide deck only, no visual demonstration

- **Metric: Team Completeness**
  - GREEN: 2-3 founders with complementary skills (technical + business), all full-time
  - YELLOW: Solo founder with strong background, or team with part-time members
  - RED: Solo non-technical founder with no technical co-founder or CTO candidate

- **Metric: Runway (Pre-Raise)**
  - GREEN: 3+ months of personal runway to dedicate full-time
  - YELLOW: 1-3 months runway, still employed part-time
  - RED: No runway, founders cannot commit full-time

- **Metric: Founder-Market Fit**
  - GREEN: Direct professional experience in the problem space (3+ years)
  - YELLOW: Adjacent experience or deep personal experience with the problem
  - RED: No clear connection to the problem space

- **Metric: Market Size (TAM)**
  - GREEN: $1B+ addressable market with credible bottoms-up analysis
  - YELLOW: $500M-$1B or top-down only estimate
  - RED: Under $500M or no market sizing done

## Typical Terms
- **Valuation ranges (pre-money):** $2M-$10M (most common: $4M-$6M for post-money SAFE cap)
- **Dilution norms:** 5-15% (target ~10%)
- **Common structures and tradeoffs:**
  - **Post-money SAFE (YC standard):** Simple, fast, founder-friendly. Dilution is clear upfront. Most common at this stage.
  - **Uncapped SAFE:** Maximum founder-friendly but increasingly rare; sophisticated angels may push back.
  - **Convertible note:** Adds a maturity date and interest rate. Some angels prefer the debt structure for tax reasons. Watch for short maturity dates (under 18 months).
- **Board seat expectations:** No board seats. Informal advisory only.
- **Pro-rata rights:** Often included in SAFE side letters for larger checks ($50K+). Not a major negotiation point.
- **Anti-dilution provisions:** Not applicable at SAFE stage.

## Key Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-optimizing for valuation.** At pre-seed, the difference between a $4M and $6M cap rarely matters in the long run. Spending months negotiating valuation instead of building product is the real cost. Take fair terms and move fast.

2. **Raising too much too early.** A $1M+ pre-seed with no product sets unrealistic expectations for the seed round. Raise just enough (6-12 months of runway) to hit the milestones that unlock the next stage.

3. **Pitching the solution before the problem.** Pre-seed investors buy into founders and problems, not features. Lead with the insight about the problem, not a product walkthrough.

4. **Neglecting founder agreements.** Not having vesting schedules, IP assignment, or a clear equity split among co-founders. Investors will ask, and ambiguity here is a red flag.

5. **Targeting the wrong investors.** Pitching Series A funds at pre-seed wastes everyone's time. Focus on angels, angel syndicates, accelerators, and dedicated pre-seed funds.

## The Narrative Shift

At pre-seed, the pitch is fundamentally about **you and your insight**.

- **Lead with:** The problem you've discovered, why it's urgent, and why you are uniquely positioned to solve it. Your unfair advantage is founder-market fit and a non-obvious insight.
- **What investors are actually evaluating:** Founder quality (intelligence, grit, speed of execution), market timing, and the size of the opportunity. They are NOT evaluating your product -- you barely have one.
- **How this differs from seed:** At seed, you'll need to show the product works and people want it. At pre-seed, you need to show that you deeply understand the problem and can move fast.

## Timeline and Process

- **When to start:** When you have a clear problem thesis, a co-founder (ideally), and can articulate a compelling vision. Many founders start while still employed, begin conversations 2-3 months before going full-time.
- **How long it typically takes:** 2-8 weeks. Pre-seed rounds are often rolling closes -- you don't need everyone to commit simultaneously.
- **Key milestones and preparation:**
  1. **Week 1-2:** Build target investor list (30-50 angels/funds). Prepare pitch deck (10-12 slides), one-pager, and a demo if possible.
  2. **Week 2-4:** Start taking meetings. Aim for 3-5 meetings per week. Get warm intros from founders in your network.
  3. **Week 3-6:** Collect soft commitments. Once you have a lead or 30%+ committed, accelerate conversations and set a deadline.
  4. **Week 4-8:** Close the round using a standard post-money SAFE. Wire funds.

## Who to Talk To

- **Types of investors active at this stage:**
  - Angel investors (current and former founders, operators)
  - Angel syndicates (AngelList, Hustle Fund syndicates)
  - Pre-seed funds ($10M-$50M fund size)
  - Accelerators (program-based investment)

- **Relevant firms and programs:**
  - **YC (Y Combinator):** $500K standard deal on post-money SAFE. Application-based. Strong network effects post-program.
  - **First Round Capital:** Occasionally invests at pre-seed for exceptional founders, especially through their Angel Track.
  - **Precursor Ventures:** Dedicated pre-seed fund, checks $250K-$1M.
  - **Hustle Fund:** Pre-seed focus, fast decision-making, $25K-$200K checks.
  - **Techstars:** Accelerator with $120K investment for 6% equity.

- **Angel groups:**
  - Founded alumni networks (YC alumni angels, Founders Fund scouts)
  - Industry-specific angel groups (fintech angels, health tech angels)
  - AngelList syndicates and rolling funds
  - Local angel networks (Golden Seeds, Band of Angels, New York Angels)
