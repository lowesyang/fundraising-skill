# YC Demo Day Deck Template

> Used by the `/pitch-deck` command. 2-minute presentation, 5-8 slides. Traction-heavy, extremely concise.

---

## Format Overview

- **Total time:** 2 minutes (strictly enforced)
- **Slides:** 5-8 (aim for 6)
- **Style:** Dark background, large fonts, minimal text, one idea per slide
- **Audience:** 500-1000+ investors watching back-to-back pitches. You must stand out in the first 10 seconds.

---

## Slide-by-Slide Template

### Slide 1: Title + One-Liner (10 seconds)
**Title:** Company Name
**Key message format:** "[Company] is [concise description in <10 words]."
**What to include:**
- Company name and logo
- One-line description that a 12-year-old could understand
- Batch identifier (e.g., "YC W26")

**What to exclude:**
- Team photos (save for end)
- Taglines or marketing fluff
- Multiple sentences

**Example:** "Acme is Stripe for insurance APIs."

---

### Slide 2: Problem + Market Size (15 seconds)
**Title:** The Problem
**Key message format:** "[Specific user] spends [time/money] doing [painful thing] because [root cause]."
**What to include:**
- The problem in one sentence, stated from the customer's perspective
- Market size — TAM, ideally a bottom-up calculation (number of potential customers x ACV)
- One number that quantifies the pain (e.g., "$40B spent annually on manual compliance")

**What to exclude:**
- Multiple problems (pick one)
- Top-down TAM ("the global X market is $Y trillion")
- More than 2 lines of text

---

### Slide 3: Solution + Demo/Screenshot (15 seconds)
**Title:** What We Built
**Key message format:** "We [verb] so that [user] can [outcome] in [timeframe/ease]."
**What to include:**
- One product screenshot or short demo clip
- One sentence explaining what the product does
- The "magic moment" — the single feature that makes users say "wow"

**What to exclude:**
- Architecture diagrams
- Feature lists
- Technical jargon

---

### Slide 4: Traction (30 seconds — this is the most important slide)
**Title:** Traction
**Key message format:** "We have [metric] growing [rate] [timeframe]."
**What to include:**
- Revenue or usage chart showing up-and-to-the-right growth
- 2-3 key metrics with specific numbers (ARR, MRR growth rate, user count, NDR)
- Growth rate prominently displayed (e.g., "28% MoM for 6 months")
- If pre-revenue: waitlist size, LOIs, pilot results with named logos

**What to exclude:**
- More than 3 metrics
- Vanity metrics (downloads, page views)
- Projections or forecasts

**Design note:** Use a simple line or bar chart. The growth line should be the dominant visual element. Make the numbers LARGE.

---

### Slide 5: Business Model (15 seconds)
**Title:** How We Make Money
**Key message format:** "We charge [who] [how much] for [what]. Current [metric] is [number]."
**What to include:**
- Pricing model in one sentence
- Current revenue or unit economics
- ACV or ARPU with supporting data
- Clear explanation of who pays

**What to exclude:**
- Multiple revenue streams (pick the primary one)
- Complex pricing tables
- Future pricing plans

---

### Slide 6: Why Now + Moat (15 seconds)
**Title:** Why Now
**Key message format:** "[Market shift/technology change] makes this possible for the first time."
**What to include:**
- One secular tailwind enabling this company now
- One defensibility angle (network effects, data moat, regulatory advantage, proprietary tech)
- Keep to 2-3 bullet points maximum

**What to exclude:**
- Competitive matrix slides
- Long lists of competitors
- Weak moats ("first mover advantage")

---

### Slide 7: Team (10 seconds)
**Title:** Team
**Key message format:** "[Names], [relevant credential]. Previously [impressive thing]."
**What to include:**
- Founder names and photos
- One line each: most relevant credential or accomplishment
- Domain expertise that makes this team uniquely suited

**What to exclude:**
- Advisors or board members
- Full work histories
- More than 3 people

---

### Slide 8: Ask (10 seconds)
**Title:** [Company Name] — [One-liner]
**Key message format:** "We are raising [amount] to [specific milestone]."
**What to include:**
- The raise amount and what it will fund
- One sentence on what the next milestone is (e.g., "to hit $3M ARR by end of year")
- Contact email
- Repeat the one-liner from slide 1

**What to exclude:**
- Valuation or terms
- Detailed use of funds breakdown
- Multiple asks

## YC-Specific Tips

### What batch partners emphasize in practice
- **Lead with traction.** If you have strong numbers, consider moving the traction slide to slide 2. YC partners will tell you: numbers are more persuasive than narrative.
- **Speed of delivery matters.** Speak quickly but clearly. Practice until the 2-minute timing is automatic. Going over time is a serious faux pas.
- **Don't explain your technology.** Explain your results. Investors at Demo Day do not have time to evaluate technical approaches.
- **The one-liner must be instantly understandable.** "[Known company] for [underserved market]" is the strongest format if it applies honestly.
- **Growth rate > absolute numbers.** $30K MRR growing 25% MoM is more fundable than $200K MRR growing 3% MoM at Demo Day stage.

### What Demo Day investors look for
- **Pattern recognition in the first 10 seconds.** If the problem and solution are not crystal clear immediately, investors mentally move on.
- **Revenue traction is king.** Usage metrics without revenue are discounted. Show revenue or a clear, immediate path to it.
- **Founder-market fit.** The team slide should make it obvious why these specific people will win this specific market.
- **Size of the opportunity.** Even at Demo Day stage, investors need to see a path to a $1B+ outcome. The market must be large enough.
- **Social proof signals.** Named customer logos, YC partner quotes, press mentions, or industry expert endorsements — any external validation helps.

### Common Demo Day mistakes
- Opening with "Hi, I'm [name] and I'm the CEO of [company]" — skip the intro, lead with the problem or traction
- Showing a roadmap slide — investors only care about what exists today
- Using animations or transitions — they break, they waste time, they look unprofessional
- Having more than 15 words on any slide — if investors are reading, they are not listening
- Mentioning competitors by name — it validates them and wastes time
