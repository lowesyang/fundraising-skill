## Salesforce Ventures

Salesforce Ventures is one of the most active corporate venture capital arms in the world, with over $5B deployed across 500+ portfolio companies. Unlike many CVCs that invest sporadically, Salesforce Ventures operates like a dedicated venture fund with a professional investment team, while maintaining deep strategic alignment with Salesforce's platform ecosystem. They invest across stages from seed to growth, with a strong preference for companies that extend and enhance the Salesforce ecosystem — AppExchange, Slack, MuleSoft, Tableau, Data Cloud, and the broader Customer 360 platform.

### Key Team

- **John Somorjai** — EVP of Corporate Development & Salesforce Ventures. The senior leader overseeing the venture arm and M&A. Sets strategic direction.
- **Alex Kayyal** — Partner, Head of EMEA. One of the most visible partners, active in European enterprise SaaS.
- **Arun Parameswaran** — Partner, focused on enterprise applications and AI.
- **Claudia Fan Munce** — Managing Partner, deep enterprise and fintech experience.
- **Rob Keith** — Partner, focused on data and analytics companies.
- **Investment Associates and Principals** — a team of ~15-20 investment professionals globally (SF, London, Tokyo, Sydney).

### Stage Focus

Seed through Growth. Salesforce Ventures is one of the few corporate VCs that genuinely invests at seed stage, though the bulk of capital goes to Series A through Growth. Seed checks are typically $1M-$3M. Their sweet spot is Series A and B ($5M-$30M). Growth-stage investments can reach $50M-$100M for strategic priorities.

### Typical Check Size

- **Seed:** $1M-$3M
- **Series A:** $3M-$15M
- **Series B:** $10M-$30M
- **Growth:** $25M-$100M

Salesforce Ventures also runs the **Salesforce Ventures Impact Fund** (focused on social impact companies) and has made dedicated AI-focused investments through thematic programs.

### Investment Thesis

Salesforce Ventures invests in companies that make the **Salesforce ecosystem more valuable**. The core thesis is platform extension: every portfolio company should ideally integrate with Salesforce's products in a way that makes the combined offering more compelling for enterprise customers.

Key thesis pillars:
- **Enterprise SaaS** — the foundation; cloud-native, subscription-based enterprise software
- **AI for CRM** — top priority now; companies building AI that enhances sales, service, marketing, and commerce workflows (aligned with Einstein GPT, Agentforce, and Data Cloud)
- **Data and analytics** — companies that enrich Salesforce's data platform (post-Tableau, post-MuleSoft)
- **Integration and automation** — middleware, iPaaS, workflow automation that connects Salesforce to the rest of the enterprise stack
- **Industry clouds** — vertical SaaS for industries Salesforce targets (financial services, healthcare, manufacturing, public sector)
- **Slack ecosystem** — apps and integrations that extend Slack's capabilities for enterprise collaboration

The strategic filter is real but not rigid. Salesforce Ventures will invest in great enterprise companies even if the Salesforce integration isn't immediate, as long as there's a plausible path to ecosystem collaboration.

### Strategic Value

Salesforce Ventures offers distribution advantages that are hard to match:

- **AppExchange distribution** — access to 150,000+ Salesforce customers through the AppExchange marketplace. This is the single most valuable asset; it can accelerate enterprise sales dramatically.
- **Joint selling with Salesforce AEs** — Salesforce's massive direct sales team (10,000+ account executives) can co-sell your product alongside Salesforce licenses. This is enterprise distribution on steroids.
- **Slack integration support** — technical and go-to-market support for building Slack apps and integrations
- **Co-marketing** — Dreamforce stage time (200,000+ attendees), Salesforce blog features, joint webinars, customer case studies
- **Technical integration support** — access to Salesforce platform engineering for deep integrations (APIs, Lightning components, Data Cloud connectors)
- **Customer intros** — warm introductions to Salesforce's enterprise customer base
- **M&A pathway** — Salesforce has acquired multiple portfolio companies; being in the portfolio creates an organic path to acquisition if that's desirable
- **Trailblazer community** — exposure to Salesforce's developer and admin community (millions of members)

### Behavioral Model

**Meeting Style:**
Professional, structured, and transparent about the strategic angle. Salesforce Ventures operates more like a traditional VC than most CVCs. Initial meetings are typically with a partner or principal who evaluates both the business fundamentals and the Salesforce ecosystem fit. Follow-up meetings bring in relevant Salesforce product team members to assess integration potential. The team is genuinely collaborative and tends to be straightforward about where they see strategic alignment (or don't). Meetings are often held at Salesforce Tower or via Zoom. The culture is polished, enterprise-professional, with Salesforce's "Ohana" (family) values visible in how they treat founders.

**Signature Questions:**
1. "How does this integrate with Salesforce today, and what does the deeper integration roadmap look like?"
2. "What does the AppExchange opportunity look like for your product? How many Salesforce customers would benefit from this?"
3. "How does this enhance the Customer 360 view? Where does your data sit relative to Salesforce's data model?"
4. "What's the AI layer in your product, and how does it complement what we're building with Einstein and Agentforce?"
5. "Tell me about your joint customers. How many of your existing customers are also Salesforce customers, and what does the overlap look like?"

**What Gets Them Excited:**
- Products already on AppExchange with meaningful traction (installs, reviews, revenue)
- AI applications that enhance CRM workflows — sales copilots, service automation, marketing intelligence
- Companies with significant Salesforce customer overlap (proves ecosystem fit without forcing it)
- Founders who understand enterprise sales cycles and have landed large logos
- Products that fill a gap in Salesforce's platform that Salesforce doesn't plan to build natively
- Strong net revenue retention (140%+) — a metric Salesforce deeply values
- Companies that could become a Dreamforce keynote story

**Red Flags:**
- Direct CRM competition (building a product that replaces Salesforce functionality)
- No Salesforce ecosystem fit — if there's no plausible integration path, the strategic case falls apart
- Consumer-focused or SMB-only products with no enterprise upmarket motion
- Companies built entirely on a competing ecosystem (Microsoft Dynamics, HubSpot-native) with no interest in Salesforce integration
- Founders who are dismissive of the strategic relationship ("we just want the money")
- Weak enterprise metrics — high churn, small deal sizes, no multi-year contracts

**Decision Process:**
3-6 weeks from first meeting to term sheet. The process involves: (1) initial partner meeting and business evaluation, (2) strategic assessment with relevant Salesforce product team, (3) investment committee review, (4) term sheet. The strategic review (step 2) is the differentiator from traditional VCs — this is where relevant Salesforce PMs and engineers assess integration potential and strategic value. For larger checks ($30M+), additional senior approvals are needed.

**Unique Quirks:**
- Salesforce Ventures is transparent about wanting strategic value. They won't pretend to be a purely financial investor. Embrace this rather than fighting it.
- Dreamforce is the ecosystem's center of gravity. Being a Salesforce Ventures portfolio company gives you access to Dreamforce stages, booths, and networking — this alone can be worth the strategic relationship.
- The team publishes an annual "Enterprise Value Creation" report and thinks deeply about enterprise SaaS metrics. Come prepared with your metrics deck.
- They have a strong ESG and impact investing arm. If your company has a social impact angle, it resonates.
- Portfolio community is active — they host events, share playbooks, and facilitate connections between portfolio companies.

### For the /pitch Simulator

**Opening Style:**
Salesforce Ventures partners open warmly and conversationally. They'll share context on what Salesforce Ventures is looking for and be upfront about the strategic lens. Expect something like: "Thanks for coming in. We've been tracking your space — really excited to learn more. Before you dive in, it's helpful to know that we look at everything through two lenses: is this a great business, and how does it fit in the Salesforce ecosystem? Walk us through your story." They give founders room to present before diving into questions.

**Push-back Patterns:**
- "I love the product, but help me see the Salesforce angle. If I put you in front of our AppExchange team, what's the pitch?" — they need to see ecosystem fit they can sell internally
- "This sounds like it overlaps with [Salesforce product]. How do you think about coexistence versus competition?" — testing whether you'll compete with Salesforce's own roadmap
- "Your enterprise metrics are early. What gives you confidence this scales to $10M+ ARR with enterprise contracts?" — they apply rigorous enterprise SaaS benchmarks
- "I see you're mostly on [competing platform] today. What would it take to go Salesforce-native?" — probing migration willingness and integration commitment
- "The AI story is compelling, but how is this differentiated from what we're building with Einstein/Agentforce?" — they want to invest in complements, not competitors to Salesforce AI

**Closing Signals:**
- Positive: "I'd love to set up a session with our [product area] team to explore the integration." Bringing in the product team is a strong buy signal.
- Positive: "Let me introduce you to [senior Salesforce exec] — I think there's a compelling story here." Escalation to senior leadership means real momentum.
- Positive: "Have you thought about Dreamforce? I could see a great customer story here." They're already thinking about how to showcase you.
- Neutral: "This is really interesting. Let us regroup internally and come back to you." Standard — not a pass, but not a strong signal.
- Negative: "I think the timing might be early for us. Let's stay in touch and revisit in 6-12 months." Polite pass, usually means ecosystem fit isn't there yet.
- Negative: "Have you explored the Salesforce partner program instead?" They're suggesting a non-investment relationship — they don't see an equity opportunity.

**Internal Discussion:**
After the pitch, the team discusses: (1) business quality — metrics, team, market size, competitive positioning, (2) Salesforce ecosystem fit — can this be an AppExchange success, does the relevant product team see value, (3) AI relevance — how does this connect to Salesforce's AI strategy, (4) strategic value to Salesforce — customer overlap, co-sell potential, acquisition optionality, (5) deal terms and ownership. The relevant Salesforce product leader's endorsement carries significant weight. If the product team is excited about integrating, the deal has strong internal momentum. If the product team is lukewarm or sees overlap with their own roadmap, the deal faces headwinds regardless of the financial case.
