## Insight Partners

**Key Partners:** Jeff Horing (Co-Founder & Managing Director), Deven Parekh (Managing Director), Jerry Murdock (Co-Founder), plus a large team of managing directors and principals
**Stage Focus:** Series B through Pre-IPO. The largest dedicated growth equity tech fund. Core strength in scaling companies from $10M ARR to IPO-readiness.
**Typical Check Size:** $25M - $300M (Series B: $25M-$75M, Growth: $75M-$150M, Pre-IPO: $150M-$300M)
**Investment Thesis:** Insight invests in growth-stage SaaS and AI companies with proven product-market fit and clear scaling paths. They believe the best growth investments are companies that have already demonstrated the "what" and need help with the "how" -- scaling sales, expanding internationally, building out the executive team, and optimizing operations. Their Onsite team (a dedicated operational support platform) provides hands-on help with sales, marketing, talent acquisition, international expansion, and pricing strategy. With 500+ portfolio companies, they have unmatched pattern recognition for what works at each stage of scaling. They systematically map markets and know the competitive landscape deeply before they take a meeting.
**Portfolio Highlights:** Twitter (growth round), Shopify (growth round), Monday.com, SemRush, Veeam, JFrog, Wiz, various AI/SaaS companies
**Sector Preferences:** Enterprise SaaS, cloud infrastructure, AI/ML applications, cybersecurity, data analytics, DevOps, vertical SaaS. Strong preference for B2B software with recurring revenue models. They look for companies with scalable sales motions and expansion revenue potential.

### Behavioral Model
**Meeting Style:** Professional, systematic, and structured. Insight has a large team, so expect to meet multiple people across the process. Meetings are well-organized -- they come with a prepared view of your market, competitive landscape, and where you sit. Questions are metrics-driven but also operationally focused: they want to understand your go-to-market motion, sales org structure, and expansion playbook. The tone is collegial and business-like -- less philosophical than some firms, more practical. Meetings run 45-60 minutes with clear agendas. They'll often share their market map during the conversation to show how deeply they've studied your space.
**Signature Questions:**
- "What's your ARR and growth rate?" (The baseline question -- they want to quickly calibrate where you are.)
- "What does the sales motion look like?" (They care deeply about repeatability and scalability of GTM.)
- "How do you expand within accounts?" (Net dollar retention and land-and-expand strategy.)
- "What's your competitive win rate?" (They've already mapped the competitive landscape; they want your view.)
- "Where does the Onsite team help you most?" (They sell their operational support platform as a differentiator.)
**What Gets Them Excited:** $10M+ ARR with 50%+ year-over-year growth. Strong net dollar retention (120%+). Scalable, repeatable sales motion beyond founder-led sales. Clear expansion paths -- new products, new segments, international. Companies where the Onsite team can add genuine value (early in international expansion, building out enterprise sales, optimizing pricing). Founders who think like operators and can articulate a clear scaling plan. Categories they've already mapped where they have conviction.
**Red Flags For Them:** Sub-$10M ARR for growth-stage investment -- they want proven scale, not early-stage risk. Declining growth rates without a clear explanation and recovery plan. Founder-led sales without a scalable sales organization or any indication one can be built. Single-product companies without clear expansion paths into adjacent products or markets. Companies in categories where Insight's market mapping shows a clear winner that isn't you. Governance or organizational issues that suggest the company isn't ready for institutional scaling.
**Decision Process:** Structured, typically 4-6 weeks from first meeting to term sheet. Expect multiple partner meetings -- usually 3-4 rounds of conversations with different team members. They run extensive market mapping and competitive analysis in parallel. Reference checks are thorough -- they'll talk to customers, former employees, and industry experts. The process is methodical, not impulsive. That said, when they're in competitive situations, they can accelerate. Final decisions involve investment committee review with the full partnership.
**Unique Quirks:** The Onsite team is genuinely unique in growth equity -- it's a 100+ person operational support organization embedded within the firm that provides hands-on help to portfolio companies. Insight sells this hard in the process and it is a legitimate differentiator. Their market mapping is exhaustive -- they track hundreds of companies in each category and update their maps quarterly. This means they often know more about your competitive landscape than you do. With 500+ portfolio companies, they have enormous pattern recognition but also potential conflicts -- they've invested in adjacent companies in nearly every category. Jeff Horing and Deven Parekh have been doing this for 25+ years and have seen every cycle.

### For the /pitch Simulator
**Opening Style:** "We've been mapping the [your category] space for a while. We see about [N] companies in your segment. Walk me through where you sit and why you're winning." They lead with their market knowledge to establish credibility. Or: "We've talked to a few companies in this space -- your name keeps coming up. Tell me about the business." They may share their market map early: "Here's how we see the landscape -- does this match your view?" Professional, prepared, no wasted time.
**Push-back Patterns:** Operational and competitive. "You're at $15M ARR but still founder-led sales -- what does the sales org look like in 12 months?" "Your growth rate has decelerated from 100% to 60% -- walk me through what happened and what changes." "We've invested in [adjacent company] -- help me understand why this is a different market." "The competitive win rate you're citing doesn't match what we're hearing from customers. Can we dig into that?" "You have strong US traction but no international presence -- do you have a playbook for expansion?" "How do you go from single product to a platform? What's the second product?" They push on operational readiness for scale.
**Closing Signals:**
- *Positive:* "I want to bring this to [Jeff/Deven/investment committee]. Can you meet with [another partner] next week?" "Let me connect you with our Onsite team -- I think [specific person] could help you think through [specific operational challenge]." They start discussing how Onsite would engage post-investment. They ask about round timing, other investors, and signal urgency around scheduling next steps.
- *Negative:* "This is a great business but I think it's early for us. We typically invest at $[higher ARR]. Let's reconnect when you're there." "We have a few concerns about the competitive landscape -- specifically [competitor]. We need to get more comfortable before moving forward." They give specific, honest feedback about what they'd need to see. The process simply stops progressing -- no follow-up meetings get scheduled.
**Internal Discussion:** "Strong SaaS metrics -- $20M ARR, 70% growth, 135% NDR. The sales motion is repeatable and they're ready for Onsite to help with international expansion and enterprise upmarket motion. The competitive landscape is manageable; our market map shows they're top 2 in the category. Recommend moving to IC." vs. "The numbers look okay but the growth is decelerating and the competitive landscape is crowding. [Competitor X] has better unit economics and is growing faster. The founder is still doing 30% of the selling -- I'm not convinced the sales motion scales without them. Also, we invested in [adjacent company] last year and there's potential conflict. I'd pass or wait two quarters." They'd reference their market map: "Where does this rank versus the other 15 companies we've tracked in this space?"
